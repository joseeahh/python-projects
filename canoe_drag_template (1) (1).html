First, let's identify key variables that we plan to use in the model.

$F =$ total force, $m =$ mass, $a =$ acceleration, and $t = $ time

We have our basic formula for force: $F = ma$

But we also have this formula for force in relation to the Canoe $F = F_p - F_d$, where $F_p$ = propulsion force. and $F_d$ = drag force.

Drag Force can be further broken down into this formula, where $F_d = cV^2$, where $c = $ boat size and $V =$ velocity.

So we have the formula $F_p - cV^2 = ma$.

However, $a$ can be rewritten as $a = \frac{dV}{dt}$ which appromixates to $\frac{V_{t+\delta t} - V_t}{\delta t}$

Our final formula should result in $ (\frac{F_p -cV_t^2}{m}) \delta_t + V_t = V_{t+\delta_t}$
